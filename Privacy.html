Privacy Policy

Effective Date: February 26, 2026

Look, hold on let me isn't a spy bot, but it does have a memory. Here is exactly what we do with your data so you don't have to wonder.

1. Data We Collect

Data Type Source Purpose
User IDs Discord API To know who is who when saving memories.
Custom Notes /remember command Stored in user_data.json so the bot can "shout" them back.
Deleted Messages Discord Gateway Processed instantly for the "Snipe Roast." We do not save these to a database.

2. How We Use Data

  • Functionality: We only store data required to make the bot's features work.
  • No Selling: We do not sell, trade, or give your data to third parties. That's weird and we're not doing it.
  • No Tracking: We don't track your activity across servers or build a "profile" on you.

3. Data Retention

Your "Memory" (from the remember command) stays in our user_data.json file until:

  • You overwrite it with a new /remember command.
  • The bot owner manually clears the database.
  • The bot is kicked from the server (this may not auto-delete data, see Section 4).

4. Your Rights (Data Deletion)

If you want us to wipe your data from the user_data.json file, you have the right to ask. Currently, you can manually clear your note by remembering something empty, or you can contact the developer directly via Discord.

5. Security

Data is stored in a JSON file on a private server. While we take reasonable steps to keep it safe, remember that no file is 100% unhackable. Don't store your passwords or credit card numbers in the /remember command—that would be a major skill issue.
